# How to plant Vetiver System

This legacy guide is kept as an archive reference, but it still carries practical planting guidance for readers who need the field sequence.

## Practical sequence

1. Confirm the site conditions and the erosion or runoff problem.
2. Prepare healthy planting material from a reliable nursery.
3. Mark contours or the layout the site calls for before planting.
4. Plant firmly so slips stay upright and keep good root contact with the soil.
5. Water, weed, and monitor early establishment.
6. Review results and adjust the layout if needed.

## Nursery and plant material

Healthy planting material matters as much as the layout. Strong slips from a reliable nursery establish faster and make it easier to get even rows, especially on slopes or other vulnerable ground.

## Planting and follow-through

The practical value of vetiver comes from the sequence, not just the plant itself. Good site reading, careful planting, and early maintenance all matter. Contour planting, regular watering, and weed control help the line settle in and start doing the work it was placed there for.

## Why the archive remains useful

Even though the live site now uses shorter public vetiver pages, this guide still helps explain the basic field logic. It remains a reference point for planting, nursery preparation, and early monitoring.
